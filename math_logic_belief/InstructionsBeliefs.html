Task:

The previous part is completed by several other people besides you. On the next two pages, we want to know what you think how well specific subgroups of people perform in the two tasks.

In total, we ask you to predict the performance of {{C.num_subtasks}} different subgroups of participants. Each subgroup consist of 100 people.
We ask you to predict...

1) ... the average score of all people of a given subgroup,
2) ... the average score of the best 10 people of a given subgroup, and
3) ... the average score of the worst 10 people of a given subgroup.

Payment:

At the end of the experiment, one of the subtasks (consisting of the three questions explained above) will be randomly selected to determine your payment in this second part of the experiment.

Your bonus payment is calculated as follows:
For each of the three questions, you receive an additional payment of $0.50 if your answer is not more than 3 points away from the correct value of the drawn sample.

Example for one subtask:

Question Your guess True value Your payment
1 28 30 $0.50 (difference between 28 and 30 is not larger than 3)
2 41 36 $0 (difference between 41 and 36 is larger than 3)
3 19 18 $0.50 (difference between 19 and 18 is not larger than 3)
Sum over the three questions: $1

If this subtask is selected to determine your bonus payment for part II, you would receive a total of $1 .
