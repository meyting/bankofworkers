In this task, you have to answer {{C.num_rounds_task}} questions, each consisting of a sequence of numbers. You have {{C.time_maths_mins}} minutes to complete all {{C.num_rounds_task}} questions. Your task is to complete the sequence by finding out which number continues the given sequence. For this, you need to figure out the pattern according to which the number sequence is constructed. The task ends either if your time is over or if you have answered all the {{C.num_rounds_task}} questions.

If you want, you can skip a question by clicking on "Next". In that case you will be directed to the next question. Please note that you cannot go back to the previous question after you have started the next question by clicking "Next". Instead of clicking on "Next" you can also press "enter" to go to the next question and to confirm your input. (This might save you some time.)

Example:

9 , 8 , 7 , 6 ,  

In this example, the rule according to which the sequence of numbers is constructed is to subtract 1 from the previous number in the sequence. Therefore, the correct answer would be "5".


Score and Payment:

For each correct answer, you get one point. You do not get any deductions for wrong or missing answers. Hence, your total score will be between 0 and {{C.num_rounds_task}}, depending on how many questions you have solved correctly. (As explained above, for each correct answer you receive {{C.bonusrate}}.)

Example:

If you answer a total of {{C.bonusexample_sequence}} out of {{C.num_rounds_task}} questions correctly, your overall score for this subtask will be {{C.bonusexample_sequence}}. The bonus payment that you would receive - if this task is drawn as the part that determines you bonus payment - is {{C.bonusexample_sequence}} * {{C.bonusrate}} = {{C.bonusexampleresult_sequence}}.
